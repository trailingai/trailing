# Market Sizing — Trailing

## Healthcare

### Deployment base
- 6,093 US hospitals (AHA Fast Facts 2025)
- ~639 health systems (AHRQ compendium 2023)
- 61% of 100 large US health systems building/implementing agentic AI or had secured budgets (Deloitte Sept 2025 survey)
- 85% planned to increase investment over next 2-3 years
- 27% of health systems and 14% of payers had implemented domain-specific AI tools (Menlo 2025)

### Estimate
~200-400 US healthcare organizations have deployed or are seriously piloting action-taking AI agents as of 2026, concentrated in large health systems and major plans.

Sources:
- AHA: https://www.aha.org/system/files/media/file/2025/01/Fast-Facts-on-US-Hospitals-2025.pdf
- Deloitte: https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/health-care/agentic-ai-health-care-operating-model-change.html

## Financial Institutions

### Deployment base
- 4,400+ FDIC-insured institutions (FDIC 2025)
- 77% of banks launched/soft-launched GenAI applications (EY 2025)
- Top challenges for agentic AI: regulatory/compliance (71%), privacy/security (67%), lack of trust (61%)

### Estimate
~300-800 US financial institutions with deployed/piloted operational AI agents beyond chatbots as of 2026.

Sources:
- FDIC: https://www.fdic.gov/quarterly-banking-profile/fdic-statistics-glance
- EY: https://www.ey.com/en_us/insights/banking-capital-markets/ai-in-banking-ey-parthenon-genai-survey-insights

## Budget Displacement

GRC budgets: 29% to GRC tools, 26% compliance audits, 24% staffing, 22% outsourcing.
44.3% of respondents estimated annual cost of compliance above $1M.
Arize median buyer pays $60K/year (Vendr benchmark).

Source: https://www.cycoresecure.com/blogs/annual-grc-budget-survey-2025-interactive-charts

## TAM Calculation

| Component | Value |
|-|-|
| Healthcare deployers | 200-400 |
| Financial deployers | 300-800 |
| Combined | 500-1,200 |
| 5% capture | 25-60 customers |
| ACV $50K-$200K | $1.25M-$12M ARR |

Near-term realistic TAM: **$1.25M-$12M ARR** across healthcare + financial services only.

Long-term (adding legal, insurance, government, broader enterprise): significantly larger, but public evidence does not support pretending this is already a $100M ARR wedge.

## Buyer Research

### Who signs off
- CISO evaluates data perimeter, network isolation, access controls
- CCO/CRO/CMIO signs off on operational validity, algorithmic fairness, auditability
- VP Engineering/CTO/Chief AI Officer when the wedge starts in tracing/evals
- Deal closes when **at least two** of these groups align

### Budget origin
- Security/cyber/IAM if sold as agent control
- Compliance/GRC/enterprise risk if sold as evidence/governance
- Engineering/AI platform if sold as tracing/observability
- Legal ops in legal-heavy organizations

### Procurement cycles
- Healthcare: 6-12 months typical
- Fintech/banks: 4-9 months
- Legal: 2-6 months
